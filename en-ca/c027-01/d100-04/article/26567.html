Connect to a network

Are you trying to connect to the web using the connection to your phone network? Check the following:

  • Is airplane mode on? Turn it off: Tap the upper-right corner of the screen, and then tap Turn off Airplane Mode.
  • Do you have a signal? If not, or if it seems weak, move to a location that has a strong signal.
  • Do you see an  or  icon in the upper-right corner of the screen? If not, make sure you have data services turned on: Quick Launch > Phone > Menu (upper left) > Preferences & Accounts > Data Usage > Enabled. If data services are on but you still don't see the icon, wait until you move to an area where the icon appears, which means that you are in an area that supports data services. Or use a Wi-Fi connection, if available (see below).
  • Are you roaming? Turn on data services while roaming: Quick Launch > Phone > Menu (upper left) > Preferences & Accounts > Data Roaming > Enabled.

Are you trying to connect using Wi-Fi? Check the following:

  • Is Wi-Fi off? Turn it on: Tap the upper-right corner of the screen, and then tap Wi-Fi > Turn on Wi-Fi.
  • Do you have a Wi-Fi signal? If not, or if it seems weak, move to a location that has a strong signal.
  • Do you need to connect to a Wi-Fi access point? Launcher > Wi-Fi > tap a network from the list. Or tap Join network and enter the necessary information (get this information from someone who knows about the access point).
  • Do you need to complete the connection by signing in? Sometimes you need to enter information about an access point on a website. Launcher > Web > enter the information.

Did you get a data connect error? Try the following:

  • Wait a few minutes and then try again.
  • Update your network settings and try again: Quick Launch > Phone > Menu (upper left) >  Preferences & Accounts > Update Network Settings (if available).
