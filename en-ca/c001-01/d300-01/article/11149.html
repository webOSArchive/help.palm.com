Set up a billing account
  1. Open App Catalog .
  2. Do one of the following:
    • Tap an app you want to buy > Download for [amount].
    • Tap Menu (upper left) > Preferences & Accounts. Under Carrier Account or Credit Card Account, tap Add Account.
  3. Enter your Palm profile password > Continue.

    If you enter an incorrect Palm profile password three times, a message appears that explains how you can contact Customer Support for help.


  4. Do one of the following:
    • To bill purchases to your wireless service account: Select the state associated with the account > confirm the email address where you want to receive purchase receipts, or enter a new address > Continue.
    • To bill purchases to your credit card: Select the country associated with your account > select a credit card type > enter your credit card and billing address details. Confirm the email address where you want to receive purchase receipts, or enter a new address > Submit. IMPORTANTIMPORTANT:

      Your credit card details are not stored on your phone. They are encrypted and stored in your Palm profile.

      Important Close
  5. Under Password Is Required, select one of the following:
    • Once every 4 hours: You must enter your password the first time you make a purchase. If you make any other purchases within four hours, you don't need to enter your password again. After four hours, you enter your password again for the first purchase, which starts a new four-hour clock.
    • Every Purchase: You must enter your password for each purchase, regardless of the time interval.
  6. Under Send Receipts, enter the email address where you want to receive app purchase receipts.
  7. If you set up both a wireless service account and a credit card account, under Default Payment Method, select the account you want to use as the default.

You can choose to bill App Catalog purchases to your wireless service account, or to a credit card account. If you enter info for more than one account, purchases are billed to the account you set as the default.
