Use Location Services
  1. Open Location Services .
  2. Tap On or Off for any of the following:

    3. Auto Locate: When Auto Locate is on, applications can automatically identify your location. When it’s off, an application asks if it’s OK to identify your location. Tap Allow to turn on Location Services for that session only.

    Geotag Photos: Available only when Auto Locate is on. When Geotag Photos is on, photos are tagged with the latitude and longitude of the location in which they were taken.

    Background Data Collection: Available only when Google Services is on. When Background Data Collection is on, anonymous location data is collected from your phone. This data is used to improve the quality of Location Services.


  3. (Optional) To turn GPS on/off: Menu (upper left) > Locate Me Using > GPS.

    4. GPS, which is on by default, provides location information to applications that request it.


  4. (Optional) To turn Google Services on/off: Menu (upper left) > Locate Me Using > Use Google Services.

    The first time you turn on Google Services you are asked to accept the terms of use, and decide whether to allow anonymous data collection. Do the following:

    • Review the terms of use.
    • If you decide not to allow anonymous data collection, tap the checkmark to remove it.
    • Tap Agree or Don't Agree.

Location Services provides information about your location to applications that request it. For example, a navigation application may need your location to give you accurate driving directions.
