Why the screen goes dark

If you hold the smartphone up to your ear while on a call, the screen goes dark. This is normal behavior. It prevents your accidentally tapping an item on the screen and saves battery power. When you take the smartphone away from your ear, the screen automatically turns back on, ready for use. If the screen doesnâ€™t turn on fast enough for you, tap the screen to wake it up.

The screen goes dark anytime you're on a call and the smartphone screen is placed near another surface. The screen turns on automatically (if the smartphone is still on) when you move the smartphone away from the other surface.

When the smartphone is on and is idle, the screen first dims and then turns off. This also is normal behavior, caused by the auto shut-off interval. You can adjust this auto shut-off interval to be as long as three minutes (learn how). At first, the screen dims as a power-saving measure. Just tap the screen to brighten it. Then, if your smartphone remains inactive for the full interval that you set, the screen turns itself off. Press the power button to turn the screen back on.

If your screen does not behave as described here, contact Support.
