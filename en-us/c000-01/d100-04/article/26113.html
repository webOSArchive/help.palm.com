Layered calendars

Suppose you use the calendar in both Microsoft Exchange and in Google. Your smartphone's Calendar app shows the events from both in a single viewâ€”a layered calendar with events that are color-coded to identify which provider they come from.

Individual events are stored separately with your different providers; the events are just assembled in one view on your smartphone for your convenience.

 

If you change an event, the change is made in the appropriate calendar.

You can also look at the events of just one calendar at a time.
