Delete files from your smartphone
  1. Connect your smartphone to your computer using the USB cable.
    Windows: If Found New Hardware messages appear on your computer, cancel them. 
  2. On your smartphone, tap USB Drive.
  3. On your computer, open the drive representing your smartphone.
  4. Use your computer's controls to navigate to and delete the files.
  5. Windows: When you're ready to disconnect, right-click the drive representing your smartphone and click Eject.

    NOTE: Do not use the Safely Remove Hardware command in the system tray to disconnect your device on a Windows computer. Use the Eject command as described here.


    Mac: From your desktop, drag the drive representing your smartphone to the Trash. Trash changes to Eject.
  6. Disconnect the USB cable from the computer when the USB Drive screen is no longer displayed on your smartphone.

Use USB Drive mode to delete files from your smartphone. You can delete any file you added to your smartphone, whether you copied it from a computer or detached it from a message.
