Copy files between your smartphone and your computer
  1. Connect your smartphone to your computer using the USB cable.
  2. On your smartphone, tap USB Drive.
  3. Windows: If Found New Hardware messages appear on your computer, cancel them.
  4. Open My Computer (Windows XP), Computer (Windows Vista/Windows 7), or the Finder (Mac), and double-click the drive representing your smartphone.
  5. Drag and drop files in either direction.
  6. Windows: When you're ready to disconnect, right-click the drive representing your smartphone and click Eject.

    NOTE: Do not use the Safely Remove Hardware command in the system tray to disconnect your device on a Windows computer. Use the Eject command as described here.


    Mac: From your desktop, drag the drive representing your smartphone to the Trash. Trash changes to Eject.
  7. Disconnect the USB cable from the computer when the USB drive screen is no longer displayed on your smartphone.

You can transfer any file to your smartphone, but you can open a file only if your smartphone has an application that can read the file.
