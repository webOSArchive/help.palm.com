Create and send a text or multimedia message

Before You Begin

  • Before you use your smartphone to send or receive multimedia messages, refer to your service plan for pricing and availability of multimedia messaging services.
  • Make sure that your smartphone is on and that you're inside a coverage area

BYB Close

  1. Open Messaging .
  2. The first time you open Messaging, tap Done to send a text or multimedia message. You can also set up an instant messaging (IM) account from this screen. Tap Add An Account (learn how).
  3. Tap . The cursor is in the To field. Do one of the following to add recipients' addresses:
    • Enter a contact first or last name or initials, screen name, phone number, or email address > tap the phone number when it appears.
    • Tap  to open Contacts and search.
    • Enter a phone number for a recipient who is not a contact.
  4. Tap the To field and repeat step 3 to enter additional recipients.
  5. To attach a picture in JPG format to a multimedia message, do one of the following:
    • Before entering the message text: Tap  > tap the album containing the picture and tap the picture, or tap New Photo and take the picture. Tap Attach Photo.
    • After entering the message text: Menu (upper left) > Add Picture > tap the album containing the picture and tap the picture, or tap New Photo and take the picture. Tap Attach Photo.
  6. To attach a video you recorded to a multimedia message, do one of the following:
    • Before entering the message text: Tap  >  > tap the album containing the video and tap the video, or tap New Video. If you tap New Video, tap  to start recording > tap  to stop > tap the new video.
    • After entering the message text: Menu (upper left) > Add Picture > > tap the album containing the video and tap the video, or tap New Video. If you tap New Video, tap  to start recording > tap  to stop > tap the new video.
  7. Enter the message text > . You can include emoticons in your message (learn how).
