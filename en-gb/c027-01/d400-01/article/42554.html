Get music onto your smartphone

You can listen to music files that you copy from your computer (learn how), receive as email attachments (learn how), receive as part of a multimedia message (learn how), buy and download from the Amazon MP3 app if it's available on your smartphone (learn how), or sync from HP Play on your computer (learn how).

You can listen to music in these file formats:

  • Files received in multimedia messages or purchased from Amazon MP3: MP3, WAV, and AMR.
  • Files copied from your computer: MP3, AAC, AAC+,WAV.
  • Files received as email attachments: All of the above, and these: AMR.

You can download and listen to MP3, WAV, and AMR files in Email and from an MMS in Messaging, but files you download from Email or Messaging are not saved to USB storage, and they are not listed in Music. MMS attachments sent to your smartphone in the WAV and AMR formats are converted and delivered to your smartphone as MP3 files.
