Connect to a captive portal
  1. Tap  at the bottom of the screen > tap the notification Captive Portal Detected on WiFi.
  2. Review the security certificate notices and, if required, tap one of the following:

    Trust Certificate: Connects you to the captive portal page automatically whenever you connect to this Wi-Fi access point.

    Trust Certificate Once: Connects you to the captive portal page for this session only.

    Don't Trust Certificate: Cancels the connection.

  3. Follow the onscreen instructions.

When connecting to some Wi-Fi access points, such as in a coffee shop, a hotel, or an airport, the notification for a captive portal sometimes appears. This means that to connect to this Wi-Fi access point, you must first go to a special Internet page, known as a captive portal, before you can connect to the Internet.

A captive portal turns a web browser into an authentication device by forcing you to go through an authentication (or sign in) web page before allowing you to access the Internet. A captive portal might be used by a Wi-Fi host to control access to the wireless network or to control the amount of time you spend connected to the host wireless network. Captive portals may also be used to request payment for Internet access.
