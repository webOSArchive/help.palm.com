View and work with notifications

Notifications show up at the top of the screen to alert you to incoming messages, upcoming events, and more. There are four types of notifications.

Banner alerts appear in a small strip at the top of the screen. These alerts confirm an action (for example, that the battery is charging) or notify you of an item you can act on (such as an incoming email message). If you do not tap a banner alert after a few seconds, it becomes an icon at the top of the screen. Tap the top of the screen or drag down over the icons to display the alerts as dashboard items.

Dashboard items provide an expanded view of notifications you can act on. When the dashboard is displayed, tap an item to act on it. A number next to a dashboard item indicates how many items are included in the notification.

Some dashboard items enable you to perform multiple actions. For example:

  • On a Calendar dashboard item, tap the calendar icon to open Calendar in Day view with the event displayed, or tap the event name to open the event details screen.
  • For a missed call, tap the icon to open your call history, or tap the missed call item to call back the number.
  • If an item's icon displays a number, tap the icon to open the associated app to the list view (such as an Email inbox), or tap the item itself to open that individual item (such as an email message).

To delete a dashboard item, swipe from left to right to throw it off the right side of the screen. If the notifications are stacked, swipe from the leftmost ("bottom") notification to dismiss all the notifications, or swipe on a single notification to dismiss just that one and display the next one in the stack.

Pop-up notifications are larger notifications that you can act on right away, such as calendar appointment notifications and alarms.

App controls let you manage an app even when it's closed. For example, if you are listening to songs in Music and leave the app, the music playback controls remain displayed at the top of the screen.

If you receive a banner alert or a pop-up notification when the screen is off, the Center button blinks. You can turn this feature off (learn how).
